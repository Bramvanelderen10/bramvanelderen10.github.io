About

My Story

My name is Bram van Elderen and I am a software engineer. I aspire to be a great game developer. I am from Waddinxveen in the Netherlands and I am currently a sofware developer for Fenêtre Online Solutions. Outside of my regular job i also develop games as an hobby, as result I’ve made multiple games and experiments of which you can see my better ones on this website.

My childhood story is probably like most gamers. I started out playing much outside and with friends, and at those friends I discovered gaming. While my family never had gaming consoles early on I could still play at my friend’s house at which my obsession over games started out and never stopped. In my teenage years I was becoming interested in how games get developed and dreamed of making games myself. Then I came across programming, I started programming my first time on a TI-84 Calculator making programs that could automatically solve mathematical problems and could give me information of all subjects where a calculator was allowed on tests. After high school I started out at the Hague University where I greatly improved my programming skills.

After my first internship I started working part time at Alphacomm which lasted for 2 years. I did learn a lot from that experience, however after that i wanted to pursue a carreer in game development. To achieve that i decided to take a break from my studies and focus soly on game development for a year. This gap year was really beneficial for not only my programming skill but also working together with random teammates on the internet and managing to succesfully complete projects in a small time frame.

Following the gap year i started working for Fenêtre, in which i started as an intern to finish my studies, and after that i stuck around since i really enjoy working at Fenêtre,
